This collection of syllogisms is living. It is the attempt of to parse the nature of photographs as works of art. They are currently unfinished, edited from time to time according to the thinking as the thinking arises.

I.

The art photograph is all that is its own situation.

II.

the art photograph is not a transmutation of actualities; it is its own actuality.

III.

the camera is not a neutral tool, but a mechanism with its own internal dialectics. the living technology of the camera informs necessarily the visual elements: shutter speed has a direct relation to the sharpness and stillness of lines, or of blurred light; aperture relates directly to depth of field, lenses can produce flatness or depth of image. these things belong properly to the camera.

IV.

THE PHOTOGRAPH CANNOT RIGHTLY BE CALLED A TRANSFORMATION OF A SCENE THROUGH PERSPECTIVE. IT CAN ONLY BE CONSIDERED THE MACHINATION OF THE CAMERA.

V.

YET, THE CAMERA IS IN CONTINUOUS RECIPROCITY WITH ITS MASTER.
