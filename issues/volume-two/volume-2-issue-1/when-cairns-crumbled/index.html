--- title: When Cairns Crumbled author: Kyle Newman previous: /issues/volume-two/volume-2-issue-1/when-there-is-mourning/ next: /issues/volume-two/volume-2-issue-1/perdition-in-c-minor/ --- 
You—camping alone in the hinterland—
don’t eat these ruby red berries
clumped together by sap. Beyond those
tail-swishing horses, over the ravine,
go past the tall pine all hikers know.
You’ll find a hackberry bush just before
the cliff, its seeds more protein-rich
than all the markets east of here.
