--- title: When Cairns Crumbled author: Kyle Newman previous: /issues/volume-two/volume-2-issue-1/when-there-is-mourning-please-dont-knock-on-my-door/ next: /issues/volume-two/volume-2-issue-1/perdition-in-c-minor/ --- {% include sqs-poem-begin.html %} You—camping alone in the hinterland— don’t eat these ruby red berries clumped together by sap. Beyond those tail-swishing horses, over the ravine, go past the tall pine all hikers know. You’ll find a hackberry bush just before the cliff, its seeds more protein-rich than all the markets east of here. {% include sqs-poem-end.html %}
